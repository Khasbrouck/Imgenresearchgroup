Travis Snyder, DOOwner and Physician Scientist:

Dr. Snyder works in many capacities: as a diagnostic radiology and neuroradiologist for SimonMed, as Director of Research and Director of Neuroradiology at SimonMed, as Assistant Program Director for the Radiology Residency Program at Mountainview Hospital, and he holds numerous academic appointments in Nevada, California, and Michigan. However, it is in his role as owner of Imgen that he can combine his far-reaching vision to advance the field of neuroradiology with his interest in improving medical care through teaching and mentoring medical students and residents. Dr. Snyder uses his experience with patients and deep understanding of current neuroradiology research to steer Imgen projects toward areas that will improve medical diagnosis and prognosis in the future.

Cheryl Vanier, PhDChief Executive Officer:

Dr. Vanier brings a diverse set of training and experience to her role as CEO of Imgen. She oversees the administrative and scientific scope of Imgen, working closely with Dr. Snyder, to discover insights that can advance the practice of neuroradiology. As a biostatistician trained in biology and experienced in complex experimental design and analysis of high-dimension data, she works with every member of the team to ensure the quality of our quantitative work and to clearly communicate our findings and their limitations to research and clinical audiences. Dr. Vanier comes from a recent stint as Chief Research Officer at Touro University Nevada, and she has been heavily involved in helping professional students, graduate students, residents, university faculty, and professional scientists design, analyze and communicate their work, producing a wealth of professional meeting presentations and 23 professional publications in the past 5 years alone, including one in Nature Communications. She has taught courses or lectured in the areas of research methods, epidemiology, biostatistics, human subjects research protections, scientific writing, introductory biology, and applied biostatistics using R software, among others.

Thomas Knoblauch, MSDirector of Research:

Thomas Knoblauch brings a strong physics background to his role as Imgen’s Director of Research, where he is involved in the development and progression of more than 40 quantitative neuroimaging studies. He handles many of the technology-related challenges of involving students and other researchers in the field of neuroradiology, and he is the main point of contact for new collaborators. One of his many projects is his own PhD project, “”. His work has resulted in numerous abstracts for professional meetings and three publications so far.

Alan Rodriguez, MDDirector of Medical Science:

Dr. Rodriguez has a passion for improving medical care through research. As Director of Medical Science at Imgen, he is responsible for providing an accurate and up-to-date medical context for the neuroradiology research projects we are pursuing. He has worked with numerous students and colleagues to produce abstracts for professional meetings and publications regarding neuroimaging findings related to carbon monoxide intoxication, Alzheimer’s disease, mild traumatic brain injury, and loss of consciousness.

Priya Santhanam, PhDResearch Scientist:

As a research collaborator, Dr. Santhanam lends support to Imgen’s efforts in neuroimaging data processing and analysis, as well as the drafting and editing of manuscripts for peer-reviewed publication. Trained in the field of biomedical imaging, with particular emphasis on magnetic resonance imaging methods and analysis, she has a strong interest in elucidating the neuronal processes underlying brain injury and disease. Over the past 10 years, she has led and contributed to multiple neuroimaging research efforts focused on patient outcomes following traumatic brain injury.
